# Product Brief

## Working title
Garden Companion

## One-line concept
A cross-platform garden app that lets a user scan a physical QR plant label and instantly open useful information about that exact plant.

## Core problem
Garden information is hard to retain and hard to share across family members. A user may not remember plant names, care requirements, or habitat value when standing in front of a plant in the garden.

## Primary use case
A person stands near a plant bed, scans a QR label attached to a plant marker, and immediately sees:
- plant name
- photo
- recognition notes
- care basics
- habitat value
- location
- planting date
- personal notes

## Initial users
- Jenny, who sets up and maintains her garden records
- Jenny's husband, who wants help learning the names and meaning of plants in the garden
- Jenny's daughter, who may maintain her own garden on Android and also later access Jenny's garden

## Product goals
- Make plant information available in context, in the garden
- Help non-gardeners learn plant names and remember plants over time
- Store practical and personal garden knowledge
- Support both iPhone and Android from the beginning
- Be simple enough to reach a working v1 and eventually be releasable

## Non-goals for v1
- automatic plant recognition from the plant itself
- AR overlays
- public plant encyclopedia
- advanced reminders and seasonal task engine
- real-time multi-user collaboration
- complex map-based layout editor

## Why QR labels are the right v1 choice
QR labels solve the exact-plant-in-exact-location problem much more reliably than visual plant identification.

Benefits:
- works year-round, even in dormancy
- works when plants are cut back or visually changed by season
- links to a specific plant instance in a specific location
- simpler and more reliable than computer vision for v1

## Product values
- useful in the real garden, not just theoretically elegant
- simple enough to finish
- local-first where possible
- respectful of cost constraints
- extensible toward future shared gardens
